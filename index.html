Break-even scenario — minimum annual fee to cover costs
Daily parking stepped $12 → $13 → $14  ·  Annual fee set to exact break-even each year
Reference scenario
This scenario answers: “If we step daily parking up by $1/year ($12 → $13 → $14), what is the minimum annual permit fee needed each year to avoid a deficit?” As the daily rate carries more revenue each year, the required annual fee decreases — reducing pressure on permit holders over time.
As daily revenue increases year over year, the break-even annual fee drops from $367 in Year 1 to $333 by Year 3 — a $34 reduction in the minimum required permit fee. The daily rate progressively subsidizes the annual permit program.
